## Session Summary
The party infiltrated a Redbrand barracks room by posing as drunken soldiers carrying an unconscious companion while singing a bawdy tavern song. When the deception failed almost instantly, violence erupted.

After defeating the Redbrands and discovering loot, the party explored the hideout further — uncovering a wizard’s workshop, a damning letter signed with a spider symbol, hidden passages, and a crypt filled with restless skeletons.

The session ended with the discovery of additional [**Redbrands**](https://github.com/Svalbaz/Neverwinter/blob/main/organisations/README.md#the-redbrands) guarding prisoners — including a blond mage who appeared to be the twin of one previously slain.

## Encounters

### Encounter 1 — The Barracks Brawl
- The party entered singing, pretending to be drunk.
- Code word established: **“STRETCH!”**
- Baras failed his lie and immediately shouted the code word.
- Baras smashed his mallet into a Redbrand’s face.
- Rurik intimidated a foe by singing:

  > “Baldy Baldy over there,  
  > What’s it like to have no hair?  
  > Is it hot or is it cold?  
  > I don’t know ‘cause I’m not bald.”

- Rurik followed this with devastating halberd damage.
- The halflings fled during the fight.
- The Boy finished the final Redbrand by cutting off his legs.

---

### Encounter 2 — The Crypt
- Skeletons rose from their coffins.
- Valen and one skeleton repeatedly missed each other for most of the fight.
- Rurik grappled a skeleton.
- Valen removed his shirt to reveal his Wood Elf tattoo, declaring his hatred of High Elves.
- Valen attempted a kerbstomp.
- Rurik destroyed a skeleton with his halberd.
- The Boy crushed the final skeleton’s skull.
- Valen put his shirt back on.
- No valuables were found within the sarcophagi.

## Exploration & Discoveries
- Wizard’s workroom containing alchemical equipment and potion-brewing texts.
- It was discovered that The Boy cannot read and responds to books with insults.
- “Iarno’s Letter” found on a desk, addressed to “Lord Albrek.”

  > Lord Albrek,  
  > My spies in Neverwinter tell me that strangers are due to arrive in Phandalin.  
  > They could be working for the dwarves. Capture them if you can, kill them if you must, but do not allow them to upset our plans.  
  > Ensure that any dwarven maps in their possession are delivered to me with haste.  
  > I'm counting on you, Iarno.  
  > Do not disappoint me.  
  > *(Signed with a Spider symbol)*

- Multiple secret doors discovered and forced open.
- Layout loops back across the crevasse to the central chamber.
- Discovery of Redbrands guarding prisoners.
- A blond mage resembling a previously slain one was present.
- Upon death, the mage claimed:

  > “I was going to be made immortal.”

## Journal

### Rurik Oathless
*Written after the events of the day.*

> We sang our way into battle like fools, and perhaps that is fitting.  
> If we are to die in some forgotten cellar beneath a ruined manor, at least we will not die quietly.
>
> Baras cannot lie to save his life. I have lost count of the times he has attempted spycraft and failed within a heartbeat.  
> The man carries the fury of his god in one hand and the subtlety of a tavern brawl in the other.
>
> The code word lasted less than a breath. “STRETCH,” he cried — and then the mallet fell.
>
> Still, steel speaks more clearly than whispers ever could.
>
> The Redbrands fought like men who believed themselves protected. One of them — a blond mage, twin to the one we slew before — spoke of immortality as he lay dying.  
> He said he was to be made immortal.
>
> I do not understand the hunger for such a thing.
>
> Even the most devout champions of Tempus are not granted eternity lightly, and never without purpose.  
> Immortality is not a prize for ambition. It is a burden reserved for those the gods would use.
>
> What is a man without the certainty of death?  
> Without an end, there is no measure of courage. No meaning in sacrifice. No weight to honour.
>
> Death is the great equaliser. It humbles kings and beggars alike.
>
> These Redbrands speak as though they have glimpsed something larger than themselves. A spider’s mark. Hidden doors. Whispers of plans.
>
> I do not like it.
>
> Steel I understand. Bone I understand. Even the rattling dead in their coffins made sense.
>
> But men who believe they cannot die?
>
> That is a different kind of danger.
