## Session Summary
The party continued their exploration of Thundertree after speaking with Reidoth, who warned them that “something wrong” might be buried beneath the town. Reidoth’s raven companion, Solas, took an interest in the group and followed them from afar.

While investigating strange spider webs and disturbed earth near the crossroads, the party discovered unsettling signs including dug-up mounds filled with fingernails and clusters of spider eggs hidden beneath the webs. Disturbing the area provoked an attack from two Giant Spiders and several Spiderlings.

After surviving the spider ambush, the group explored more of Thundertree and investigated a statue of the legendary hunter Halian. The party then encountered a ruined barracks infested with Ash Zombies. Using clever positioning and teamwork, they barricaded the entrance and fought the undead in the doorway, eventually clearing the building.

Inside the barracks, the party uncovered weapons, armour, valuables, and what appeared to be Halian’s personal chambers, including an ornate Boar Hunting Spear mounted above a desk. After securing the building, the group barricaded the barracks and settled in for a Long Rest.

### Kuraken Goldkettle
*Written after the events of the day.*

> Spiders, why did it have to be Spiders?
> 
> I truly, deeply, passionately hate spiders.  
>
> Today began with Reidoth speaking in riddles, warning us that something foul sleeps beneath Thundertree. Naturally, moments later, The Boy started digging random holes in the dirt and discovered fingernails. Fingernails.  
>
> Before I could even begin processing that, giant spiders descended upon us from the webs. One wrapped me up like a kebab and bit straight through my armour. I have never screamed so loudly in my life.  
>
> Cal somehow managed to make the situation worse by exploding spider eggs and releasing even more spiders. Smaller ones this time, but even so they were the size of dinner plates. One of them tried to bite me and I retaliated with my frying pan. I missed.  
>
> The Boy, meanwhile, hacked creatures apart while Hashon fought with the confidence of a man completely unconcerned by death, venom, or common sense.  
>
> We later found an old barracks filled with ash-covered undead. Hashon held the doorway while the rest of us stabbed over and around him. I am fairly certain I spent half the battle attacking enemies through his legs.  
>
> I now possess a fine Silver Case with an ornate Quill, a cooked spiderling and a few more Gold Coins.
>
> Venomfang stills awaits. I've never seen a Dragon before, I wonder how they taste.
